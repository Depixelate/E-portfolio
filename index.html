Course Learning Outcomes

Throughout ENC 1102, I have developed skills in the following key areas:

1. Generating Inquiry

This outcome focuses on the ability to ask genuine, complex questions. It involves developing a line of inquiry to explore topics related to writing, rhetoric, or literacy, rather than just reporting known facts.

2. Multiple Ways of Writing

This outcome asks students to integrate different modes of communication. It means understanding how to use text, visuals, layout, and different media formats to support an argument effectively.

3. Information Literacy

This involves the ability to evaluate sources for credibility, relevance, and ethics. It is about understanding where information comes from and how to use primary and secondary sources responsibly.

4. Research Genre Production

This outcome is about producing writing that fits specific public or academic contexts. It demonstrates the ability to navigate the rules, choices, and constraints of different research genres.

5. Contributing Knowledge

Rather than just summarizing others, this outcome requires drawing original conclusions based on primary evidence. It involves placing your own analysis in conversation with existing research.

6. Revision

This outcome highlights writing as a process. It demonstrates the ability to negotiate differences, accept feedback, and act with intention to rethink and improve writing through multiple drafts.

Final Research Paper

Commit as Genre: The structure and function of commit messages across three GitHub projects

The goal of this final paper was to treat commit messages as a recurring kind of writing. It asks what parts these messages usually have, what parts sometimes appear, what each part does for readers, and how message form differs in different kinds of projects. The goal is to produce clear, practical descriptions that readers with no programming background can understand.
